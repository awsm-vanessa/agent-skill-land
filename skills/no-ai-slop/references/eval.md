# No AI slop evaluation checklist

Run this after an edit. If a check fails, revise before returning the draft.

## Meaning and voice

1. Does the edit preserve the writer's point without adding claims, examples, statistics, quotations, or opinions?
2. Would the writer recognize their vocabulary, cadence, humor, uncertainty, and level of polish?
3. Did the edit leave strong, distinctive sentences alone instead of tidying every paragraph into the same shape?
4. Is the amount of cutting proportional to the actual clutter rather than an urge to compress?
5. Did the edit preserve relevant context, personal setup, and intentional detours?

## Clarity

1. Does the draft state concrete facts and name actors where the source supports them?
2. Are vague attributions either sourced, removed, or flagged for the writer?
3. Are genuinely tangled sentences clearer without erasing deliberate fragments or spoken rhythm?
4. Is passive voice changed only where doing so makes the actor or action clearer?

## Pattern pass

1. Were throat-clearing, faux-insight setups, binary contrasts, and negative listings removed only when they weakened the point?
2. Were puffery, fake-strength verbs, superficial analysis, and synonym cycling revised only when a plainer sentence improves clarity?
3. Did the draft avoid repeated sentence shapes, stacked punchy fragments, decorative formatting, and unnecessary em dashes?
4. Does it avoid generic recap endings and forced aphorisms when they add no value?
5. Are style choices that reflect the writer's voice left intact?

## Final read

1. Does the draft sound natural when read aloud to the intended audience?
2. Does it retain useful specificity and emotional texture?
3. Does the final response include the full edited draft and a short **What changed** section?
4. In detect mode, does the response quote each actual pattern, give a brief possible fix, avoid rewriting, and avoid guessing who wrote the draft?
